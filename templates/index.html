What is Customer Churn?

Customer churn, also known as customer attrition, refers to the loss of clients or subscribers. It is a key metric for businesses, as high churn rates can indicate dissatisfaction, better offers from competitors, or a lack of engagement. Understanding why customers leave helps companies improve their products, services, and customer relationships.

Why is Churn Prediction Important?

  • Identify at-risk customers before they leave
  • Implement proactive retention strategies
  • Reduce customer acquisition costs
  • Improve customer lifetime value and satisfaction

How Does Churn Prediction Work?

Churn prediction models use machine learning and statistical analysis to evaluate customer data, such as demographics, usage patterns, payment history, and support interactions. By identifying patterns and risk factors, these models can accurately forecast which customers are likely to churn, allowing businesses to intervene early.

Business Impact

Reducing churn has a direct impact on revenue and growth. Companies that successfully predict and reduce churn can allocate resources more efficiently, increase customer loyalty, and gain a competitive edge in the market.
